# Extending Situational Strength Theory to Account for Situation-Outcome Mismatch (Dalal et al., 2020)

## Hypotheses
*   **Hypothesis 1:** The impact of personality traits on counterproductive work behavior is moderated—specifically, weakened—by counterproductive-work-behavior-focused situational strength.
*   **Hypothesis 2:** The impact of personality traits on counterproductive work behavior is moderated—specifically, strengthened—by task-performance-focused situational strength.
*   **Hypothesis 3:** The moderating (specifically, strengthening) effect of task-performance-focused situational strength on the relationship between personality traits and counterproductive work behavior is itself mediated by negative affect.
*   **Hypothesis 4:** The moderated mediation model proposed in Hypothesis 3 is further qualified, such that the second stage of the mediation—namely, the relationship between negative affect and counterproductive work behavior—is moderated (specifically, weakened) by counterproductive-work-behavior-focused situational strength.

## Methodology
*   **Sample:** 369 participants (54% US, 46% India) recruited through Amazon Mechanical Turk (MTurk). The sample consisted primarily of professionals (41%), managers (18%), clerical or office workers (13%), and technicians (11%).
*   **Design:** A two-wave survey design with a two-week interval between the timepoints. Demographic variables and personality traits were measured at time 1. Task-performance-focused situational strength was measured at time 1 and time 2. CWB-focused situational strength, negative affect, and CWB were measured at time 2.
*   **Measures:** 
    *   *Personality:* Agreeableness, conscientiousness, and emotional stability were measured using the Big Five Inventory (BFI).
    *   *Negative Affect:* Measured via the Positive and Negative Affect Schedule (PANAS).
    *   *Counterproductive Work Behavior (CWB):* Organizationally directed CWB (CWBO) and interpersonally directed CWB (CWBI) were measured using self-reports from Bennett and Robinson's (2000) workplace deviance measure.
    *   *CWB-Focused Situational Strength:* Measured using an adapted version of the CWB scale, asking the extent to which the organization discourages or encourages specific CWB behaviors.
    *   *Task-Performance-Focused Situational Strength:* Measured using an adapted version of Meyer et al.'s (2014) situational strength at work (SSW) scale, specifically focusing on the facets of clarity and constraints.
*   **Analysis:** Hypotheses were tested using simple moderation and dual-stage moderated mediation analysis via the PROCESS macro in SPSS.

## Empirical Findings
*   **Hypothesis 1:** Fully supported. CWB-focused situational strength significantly weakened the relationship between all three personality traits (agreeableness, conscientiousness, and emotional stability) and both forms of CWB (CWBO and CWBI). 
*   **Hypothesis 2:** Mostly supported for agreeableness, fully supported for conscientiousness, and unsupported for emotional stability. Task-performance-focused situational strength (clarity and constraints) generally strengthened the negative relationship between personality traits (agreeableness and conscientiousness) and CWB.
*   **Hypothesis 3:** Mostly supported in the case of agreeableness and conscientiousness, but unsupported for emotional stability. The first-stage interaction term indicated that the negative relationship between personality traits and negative affect was stronger when task-performance-focused situational strength was high.
*   **Hypothesis 4:** Fully supported. The second-stage interaction indicated that the negative relationship between negative affect and CWB (either CWBO or CWBI) was stronger when CWB-focused situational strength was high. 

## Stated Limitations
*   The study relied exclusively on self-report measures, which raises the possibility of common-method variance (specifically, same-source bias) and therefore inflated relationships.
*   The research design employed two timepoints, meaning the mediator (negative affect) and outcomes (CWBO and CWBI) were not separated temporally. This limits the ability to draw strong causal inferences compared to a three-timepoint cross-lagged design.
