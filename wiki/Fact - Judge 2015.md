# The Person–Situation Debate Revisited: Effect of Situation Strength and Trait Activation on the Validity of the Big Five Personality Traits in Predicting Job Performance

**Authors:** Timothy A. Judge, Cindy P. Zapata
**Year:** 2015

## Explicit Hypotheses
*   **Hypothesis 1:** The relationship of the Big Five traits (conscientiousness, emotional stability, extraversion, agreeableness, and openness) with job performance will be stronger (more positive) in occupations in which situation strength—in terms of (a) the outcomes of what work is done and (b) the process of how the work is done—is low (i.e., in weak situations).
*   **Hypothesis 2:** The conscientiousness–job performance relationship will be stronger (more positive) in (a) occupations requiring independence and (b) occupations with strong attention-to-detail requirements.
*   **Hypothesis 3:** The emotional stability–job performance relationship will be stronger (more positive) in (a) occupations requiring strong social skills and (b) occupations in which one must frequently deal with unpleasant or angry people.
*   **Hypothesis 4:** The extraversion–job performance relationship will be stronger (more positive) in (a) occupations requiring strong social skills, (b) occupations with a strong level of competition requirement, and (c) occupations in which one must frequently deal with unpleasant or angry people.
*   **Hypothesis 5:** The agreeableness–job performance relationship will be (a) stronger (more positive) in occupations requiring strong social skills, (b) weaker (less positive) in occupations with a strong level of competition requirement, and (c) stronger (more positive) in occupations in which one must frequently deal with unpleasant or angry people.
*   **Hypothesis 6:** The openness–job performance relationship will be stronger (more positive) in (a) occupations requiring independence and (b) occupations with strong innovation requirements.

## Methodologies
*   **Literature Search:** A three-part search process (manual search of previous meta-analyses, PsycINFO database search, and reverse citation search) identified 125 codable studies examining the relationship between the Big Five traits and job performance in natural job settings within single occupations.
*   **Data Coding:** Coded for personality trait, sample size, validity coefficients, reliabilities, and occupation. O*NET data was used to code for six situation strength facets and six trait activation factors.
*   **Reliability Assessment:** Administered an online survey to 81 organizational behavior researchers who evaluated 12 job context variables across 12 job titles to establish the reliability of O*NET ratings.
*   **Data Analysis:** Utilized principal components analysis to form situation strength composite variables (outcomes and process). Bootstrapped constrained nonlinear regression analysis (1,000 regressions) was used to predict personality-performance validity coefficients based on job context variables. Dominance analysis and relative weight index were used to compare the relative importance of situation strength versus trait activation frameworks.

## Empirical Findings
*   **Situation Strength:** Situation strength—process significantly positively predicted the validity coefficients for all five traits, indicating higher validities in weak situations. Situation strength—outcomes negatively predicted the validities only for agreeableness and openness, but not for conscientiousness, emotional stability, or extraversion.
*   **Trait Activation (Conscientiousness):** Independence requirement positively predicted the conscientiousness-job performance relationship (supporting H2a). However, attention to detail requirement negatively predicted this relationship (failing to support H2b).
*   **Trait Activation (Emotional Stability):** Social skills and dealing with unpleasant/angry people requirements positively predicted the emotional stability-job performance relationship (supporting H3a and H3b).
*   **Trait Activation (Extraversion):** Social skills, level of competition, and dealing with unpleasant/angry people requirements positively predicted the extraversion-job performance relationship (supporting H4a, H4b, H4c).
*   **Trait Activation (Agreeableness):** Social skills and dealing with unpleasant/angry people requirements positively predicted the agreeableness-job performance relationship (supporting H5a and H5c). Competition requirement negatively predicted this relationship (supporting H5b).
*   **Trait Activation (Openness):** Independence and innovation/creativity requirements positively predicted the openness-job performance relationship (supporting H6a and H6b).
*   **Unexpected Findings:** Conscientiousness more positively predicted performance in jobs with strong innovation/creativity requirements and those involving unpleasant/angry people. Extraversion was a negative predictor of performance in jobs with strong attention to detail. Agreeableness positively predicted performance in jobs requiring attention to detail and independence.
*   **Relative Importance:** Dominance weights and relative weight indices indicated that trait activation variables were generally more important than situation strength variables in explaining personality-performance relationships for four of the five traits (the exception being openness, where situation strength was more important).

## Stated Limitations
*   The study does not exhaust the list of trait-relevant cues that might moderate personality-job performance relationships (e.g., social factors, human resource systems, organizational culture).
*   The model focused on the degree to which personality traits express themselves in overall job performance, rather than in specific job behaviors that lead to performance.
*   Conceptualizing job characteristics at the occupational level conflates three sources of variation: the occupation, the organization, and the idiosyncratic nature of the work itself.
*   The study only utilized "alpha press" (objective characteristics of an occupation) rather than "beta press" (job conditions uniquely perceived by an individual).
*   The interactionist model relied on unconditional conceptualizations of traits and did not consider how traits and situations may mutually affect one another over time or specific "behavioral signatures."

## Core Variables Measured
*   Big Five Personality Traits: Conscientiousness, Emotional Stability, Extraversion, Agreeableness, Openness
*   Job Performance (overall or task performance)
*   Situation Strength (Outcomes): Impact of decisions on coworkers/results, Consequences of error, Responsibility for health/safety of others
*   Situation Strength (Process): Unstructured (vs. structured) work, Freedom to make decisions, Variety
*   Trait Activation Variables: Independence in completing work, Attention to detail requirement, Social skills requirement, Level of competition requirement, Innovation/creativity requirement, Dealing with unpleasant or angry people
