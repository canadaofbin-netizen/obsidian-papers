# Concept: Interactionist Model (상호작용론 모델)

## 📌 Definition
The **Interactionist Model** posits that human behavior and job performance are determined neither solely by internal personality traits nor entirely by external environments, but by the complex interaction between the two.

It typically bridges two major theories:
1. **[[Concept - Situational Strength (상황 강도)]]**: General environmental cues that restrict or allow personality to predict behavior.
2. **Trait Activation Theory**: Specific environmental cues that "activate" specific traits (e.g., a job requiring high social skills activates Extraversion).

## 🔗 Semantic Connections (Cross-References)
- **Predicting Performance**: [[Source - Judge (2015)]] elegantly integrates these two theories, proving that situational strength acts as a general dampener on all traits, whereas trait activation acts as a specific catalyst for individual traits.
- **Meta-Analytic Evidence**: The validity of the interactionist model is strongly supported by the meta-analysis in [[Source - Meyer (2009)]], which showed that the Conscientiousness-Performance link fluctuates massively based on occupational constraints.
