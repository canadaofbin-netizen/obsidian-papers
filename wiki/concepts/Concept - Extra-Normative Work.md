# Concept: Extra-Normative Work

## 📌 Definition
**Extra-Normative Work** refers to work executed outside the standard physical or temporal boundaries expected in a traditional job role. Examples include answering emails late at night, weekend work, or abrupt shifts to mandatory teleworking (like during the COVID-19 pandemic).

## 🔗 Semantic Connections (Cross-References)
- **The Strain Paradox**: According to [[Source - Calderwood (2023)]], when extra-normative work is enforced as a "strong situation" (e.g., severe consequences for missing after-hours emails), it leads to extreme emotional exhaustion and anxiety. 
- **Mitigation via Clarity**: Interestingly, if the [[Concept - Situational Strength]] comes from *Clarity* and *Consistency* rather than *Constraints*, extra-normative work actually causes *less* strain, as employees know exactly what is expected of them outside normal hours.
