# Concept Hub: Macro-Occupational vs. Micro-Episodic Disconnect

## Latent Correlation
There is a fundamental methodological and theoretical divide in the literature regarding how situational strength is measured and experienced by the employee: the difference between objective occupational averages (alpha press) and individual episodic perceptions (beta press).

## Key Intersections
*   **Meyer (2009) & Judge (2015):** Both meta-analyses rely on O*NET databases to assign a single, static "Situational Strength" or "Trait Activation" score to an entire occupation (e.g., Surgeon, Barber). Judge acknowledges this as a limitation (using alpha press).
*   **Dalal (2020) & Calderwood (2023):** Both utilize self-reports of the immediate, perceived environment (beta press). Calderwood specifically focuses on transient, episodic extra-normative events (e.g., an after-hours email), while Dalal measures specific perceived organizational attitudes toward CWB.

## Theoretical Synthesis
The objective parameters of a job (O*NET data in Meyer/Judge) only set the baseline boundary conditions. The actual behavioral outcomes (strain in Calderwood, CWB in Dalal) are driven by transient, micro-episodic fluctuations in situational strength that cannot be captured by occupational averages. The future of situational strength research must bridge the gap between static job descriptions and dynamic psychological experiences.
