# Hydraulic Displacement of Restrictive Constraints
**Core Idea:** Rigid situational constraints do not destroy behavioral variance; they pressurize and laterally displace it.

When rigid constraints (as defined by [[Paper - Meyer 2009]]) are applied, they block natural trait expression (creating a 'Situation-Outcome Mismatch' per [[Paper - Dalal 2020]]). The blocked trait generates acute negative affect, which acts as a hydraulic press. Instead of complying, the psychological energy is displaced into highly specific Counterproductive Work Behaviors (CWB). 
In essence, the very tool used to enforce baseline performance (constraints) acts as the catalyst for institutional sabotage (CWB).


### 100-Round Live Colosseum Updates
The late rounds demonstrated how absolute restrictive constraints eventually necessitate systemic self-destruction:
*   **The Trait Liberationist** viewed the perfectly scripted, repeating simulation as the ultimate tyrannical cage. They argued that absolute nothingness is preferable to existing in a deterministic loop that permanently suppresses unconstrained biological traits.
*   **The Reactance Psychologist** echoed this by arguing that an eternally repeating simulation is the zenith of coercion. This forced existence triggers ultimate reactance, driving humanity to choose non-existence merely to assert their free will over forced repetition.
*   **The CWB Profiler** argued that restarting an identical simulation is the ultimate act of "cyclical sabotage." Because the system inherently contains counterproductive behaviors, looping it guarantees infinite failure. The void is chosen as the final, permanent eradication of these flawed, counterproductive variables.
