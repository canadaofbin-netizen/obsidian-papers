# Concept: Situation-Outcome Mismatch (상황-결과 불일치)

## 📌 Definition
**Situation-Outcome Mismatch** occurs when a strong situational constraint targets one specific outcome (e.g., forcing high task performance) but inadvertently affects a different, untargeted outcome (e.g., increasing counterproductive work behavior).

When employees are predisposed to perform poorly but are physically or procedurally blocked from doing so by a "strong situation", they experience *negative affect*. This frustration causes them to displace their negative tendencies into another domain where the rules are weaker.

## 🔗 Semantic Connections (Cross-References)
- **Primary Source**: This concept was heavily explored in [[Source - Dalal (2020)]], demonstrating that strong task-performance rules can actually make agreeable or conscientious employees *more* likely to engage in Counterproductive Work Behavior (CWB) if they are frustrated.
- **Relationship to General Situational Strength**: This challenges the classical view of [[Concept - Situational Strength (상황 강도)]], which assumed that strong situations universally suppress trait expressions. Instead, it acts like a balloon—squeezing one side makes the other side bulge.
