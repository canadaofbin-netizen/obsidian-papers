# Concept Hub: The Consequences-Strain-Deviance Pipeline

## Latent Correlation
A critical intersection emerges when connecting the macro-level "Consequences" of situational strength with individual-level emotional exhaustion and subsequent counterproductive work behaviors (CWB).

## Key Intersections
*   **Meyer (2009) & Judge (2015):** High "Consequences of error" are categorized as defining features of strong situations. Meyer shows this restricts the variance in task performance driven by conscientiousness.
*   **Calderwood (2023):** Demonstrates that when extra-normative work situations have high "Consequences," they directly trigger emotional exhaustion, anxiety, and perceived job stress. 
*   **Dalal (2020):** Establishes that Negative Affect acts as a mediator between personality traits and CWB, particularly when task-performance situational strength is high.

## Theoretical Synthesis
High-stakes environments (high consequences) restrict variance in positive performance (Meyer 2009) but simultaneously generate intense psychological strain (Calderwood 2023). This strain manifests as Negative Affect, which then becomes the primary engine for Counterproductive Work Behaviors (Dalal 2020), especially if the organizational culture lacks specific CWB-focused situational constraints. Thus, over-constraining employees for task performance without managing strain can inadvertently trigger deviance.
