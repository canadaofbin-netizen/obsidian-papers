# Concept Hub: Target-Specific Situational Strength and Trait Activation

## Latent Correlation
Historically, situational strength was viewed as a uniform, global suppressor of personality (e.g., Meyer et al., 2009). However, latent correlations across later literature reveal that situational strength is highly specific to the target behavior (Dalal et al., 2020) and interacts intimately with Trait Activation Theory (Judge & Zapata, 2015). 

## Key Intersections
*   **Meyer (2009) vs. Dalal (2020):** Meyer found that high constraints globally weakened the conscientiousness-performance link. Dalal refined this by showing a *mismatch* effect: task-performance-focused situational strength can actually *strengthen* the negative relationship between traits and Counterproductive Work Behaviors (CWBs).
*   **Judge (2015) & Trait Activation:** Judge's findings demonstrate that specific job demands (e.g., social skills, dealing with angry people) activate specific traits (emotional stability, agreeableness) to predict performance, proving that "strength" isn't just about suppressing behavior, but directing it.

## Theoretical Synthesis
Situations do not uniformly constrain behavior; they channel it. A situation can be "strong" for task performance but "weak" for CWB (Dalal 2020), allowing latent traits activated by specific demands (Judge 2015) to express themselves in divergent ways, rendering global occupational strength measures (Meyer 2009) too blunt to capture organizational reality.
