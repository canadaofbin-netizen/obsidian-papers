# Calderwood et al. (2023) Extra-normative work and strain

## Hypotheses
* **Hypothesis 1:** Employees can distinguish between weak and strong extra-normative work situations.
* **Hypothesis 2:** Aggregate extra-normative situational strength is positively associated with extra-normative work demand compliance.
* **Hypothesis 3:** Aggregate extra-normative situational strength is positively associated with employee strain.
* **Hypothesis 4:** Extra-normative (a) consistency and (b) clarity are negatively associated with employee strain.
* **Hypothesis 5:** Extra-normative (a) consequences and (b) constraints are positively associated with employee strain.

## Methodologies
* **Study 1:** A within-subjects experimental vignette study with 243 participants recruited via Prolific Academic. Participants were randomly assigned to view a Weak or Strong manipulation for three extra-normative work situations (meeting spillover, office party, post-work e-mail). Used random coefficients regression to evaluate the effects of aggregate situational strength on intended behavior and strain.
* **Study 2:** A cross-sectional online survey of 436 Amazon MTurkers working full-time in non-executive jobs with standard business hours, but who occasionally worked outside these hours. Used structural equation models to simultaneously predict strain criteria from the four facets of situational strength.
* **Study 3:** A cross-sectional online survey of 290 full-time employees recruited via Prolific Academic who had abruptly transitioned to teleworking every day in response to the COVID-19 pandemic. Used structural equation models to simultaneously predict strain criteria from the four facets of situational strength.

## Empirical Findings
* **Study 1:**
  * Participants perceived the situational strength of extra-normative work to be higher in the Strong condition than in the Weak condition (Hypothesis 1 supported).
  * Exposure to the Strong condition caused increases in predicted behavioral compliance, emotional exhaustion, and anxiety, relative to the Weak condition (Hypotheses 2 and 3 supported).
* **Study 2 (After-Hours Work):**
  * Consistency was associated with lower emotional exhaustion, work anxiety, and perceived job stress (Hypothesis 4a supported).
  * There was no evidence linking clarity to strain criteria (Hypothesis 4b unsupported).
  * Consequences co-varied with higher emotional exhaustion, work anxiety, and perceived job stress (Hypothesis 5a supported).
  * Constraints were positively related to emotional exhaustion, but not to work anxiety or perceived job stress (Hypothesis 5b partially supported).
* **Study 3 (COVID-19 Telework):**
  * There was no evidence that consistency negatively co-varied with any strain criteria (Hypothesis 4a unsupported).
  * Clarity was associated with lower perceived job stress, work anxiety, and emotional exhaustion (Hypothesis 4b supported).
  * Consequences were related to higher perceived job stress, work anxiety, and emotional exhaustion (Hypothesis 5a supported).
  * Constraints were linked to higher emotional exhaustion, but not to perceived job stress or work anxiety (Hypothesis 5b partially supported).

## Stated Limitations
* All variables were measured via self-reports, which are subject to biases and distortions that may inflate measurement error.
* Data in all three studies were collected at a single point in time, leaving the researchers unable to establish how stable extra-normative situational strength is over time or how within-person variability may impact shorter-term strain criteria.

## Core Variables
* Extra-normative situational strength (Facets: Consistency, Clarity, Consequences, Constraints)
* Extra-normative work demand compliance / Behavioral compliance
* Employee strain (Measured via: Emotional exhaustion, Work anxiety, Perceived job stress)
